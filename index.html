Post-COVID Demographic Reversion

Following the unprecedented peak of 16.3M beneficiaries in 2022 (driven by a massive 52% influx in the 25–44 working-age bracket), the claimant base has normalized back toward its historical distribution. By 2024, the seniors (65+) share rebounded from a low of 41.5% back up to 47.0%, closely matching their pre-pandemic baseline of 47.9%. The working-age influx has largely receded, though overall system volume (14.9M) remains structurally elevated compared to 2020.

Methodology & Data Integrity Guide

GDocP Compliant | Ref: GD-CAN-DUMD-001

1. Research Scope & Monitored Substances

The system tracks high-risk therapeutic classes using WHO ATC codes to support public health monitoring of opioid analgesics and central nervous system stimulants:

  • N02AB03 (Fentanyl): Synthetic opioid analgesic. Major contributor to opioid overdose metrics.
  • N02AA03 (Hydromorphone): Potent semi-synthetic opioid. Heavily regulated.
  • N02AA05 (Oxycodone): Moderate-to-high potency opioid analgesic.
  • N06BA04 (Methylphenidate): CNS stimulant utilized for ADHD management. Significant adult prescription growth.
  • N06BA12 (Lisdexamfetamine): Prodrug stimulant showing rapid market penetration.

Time Horizon: Annual data from 2020 – 2024. All data represents audited figures sourced from CIHI or ISC directly.

2. Data Sources & Methodology

2.1 Drug-Specific Public Spending (Primary Indicator)

Drug-level public spending data is sourced directly from the CIHI NPDUIS database tables (Table 3 — High-Cost Drugs). This provides actual annual public drug program spending in millions of CAD, broken down by:

  • ATC5 Code — Specific drug identification (WHO classification)
  • Jurisdiction — 11 Canadian jurisdictions (10 provinces + Yukon)
  • Calendar Year — 2020 through 2024

Year-over-year percentage change is calculated as:

YoY Change (%) = ((Spending_t − Spending_{t-1}) / Spending_{t-1}) × 100

2.2 Overall Plan-Level Metrics

Aggregate public drug plan spending and beneficiary counts are sourced from CIHI Table 1 (Prescribed Drug Spending). These are not drug-specific — they represent the overall public drug program profile:

  • Annual Public Spending: Total public pharmacy drug spending by jurisdiction ($ millions CAD).
  • Active Beneficiaries: Total number of distinct active beneficiaries who received at least one plan payout.
  • Per-Client Plan Average: Total annual spending ÷ total active beneficiaries.

2.3 NIHB Federal Program Data

The federal Non-Insured Health Benefits (NIHB) program is administered by Indigenous Services Canada (ISC). Drug-specific NIHB claims data is restricted under OCAP® principles (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession). Program-level totals are sourced from official NIHB Annual Reports:

  • 2020: 31.73M total pharmacy claims, 537.6K clients, $536.7M expenditures
  • 2021: 31.19M claims, 549.5K clients, $566.1M expenditures
  • 2022: 30.49M claims, 559.4K clients, $605.8M expenditures
  • 2023: 29.50M claims, 585.1K clients, $648.6M expenditures
  • 2024: Projected based on historical trend (estimated)

4. ALCOA+ Adherence & Audit Trail

Attributable

Pipeline execution is automated via Python scripts logged to the system. Source downloads are tagged with date and author roles.

Legible

Structured JSON output maps cleanly to ATC codes and date keys, preventing transcription errors.

Contemporaneous

Each pipeline run creates a timestamped audit record showing the exact date/time of execution.

Original

Raw unaltered CIHI Excel source files are stored securely under data/raw/.

Accurate

All spending figures are extracted directly from CIHI audited data tables. No simulated values are used for drug-level metrics.
